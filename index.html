The Gap
Student musicians have no real stages.
Most play once a year at a recital — graded, evaluated, artificial. Meanwhile, senior centers, hospitals, and care facilities are full of people who rarely experience live music. TAP closes both gaps simultaneously.
The Mission
Live music as neurological intervention.
Every performance decision — venue selection, audience type, setlist, tempo — is informed by what neuroscience says music does to the brain. This isn't community service dressed up. It's intentional delivery of a documented neurological benefit.

Why Neuroscience
Music &
the Mind
TAP doesn't perform randomly. Every venue is chosen because neuroscience documents what live acoustic music does to that specific population — which neural pathways it activates, which symptoms it alleviates, which connections it restores. The performance is the delivery mechanism. The science is the rationale.
🧠
Memory & Dementia
Musical memory is stored in a separate neural region from other memory — it survives deeper into cognitive decline than almost anything else. Familiar songs reach people who can no longer recognize their own family.
Hippocampal independence
🎵
Autism & Development
Music reduces anxiety and improves social communication in children with developmental disabilities. It activates mirror neuron systems and social bonding pathways that other stimuli don't reach.
Mirror neuron activation
⚡
Parkinson's & Motor
Rhythmic auditory stimulation is a documented clinical technique for Parkinson's patients — rhythm from music helps regulate motor control. TAP's setlist choices for motor-affected audiences reflect this directly.
Rhythmic auditory stimulation
💜
Mental Health
Live music reduces cortisol and triggers dopamine release. Shared musical experience creates neural synchrony — during live performance, the brains of audience members literally synchronize with each other.
Neural oscillatory coupling
Process How It Works
Step 01
Identify the Audience
TAP targets populations where neuroscience documents the highest music impact — dementia patients, children with disabilities, mental health communities, rehabilitation patients. Venue selection is intentional, not opportunistic.
Step 02
Book the Stage
Direct outreach to venues. TAP handles all logistics — scheduling, orientation, health clearances — from scratch. Every venue follows the same template that got ActivCare on a monthly schedule.
Step 03
Perform with Intention
30–45 minute acoustic sets. Setlists built around the specific audience — familiar songs for memory care, rhythmic pieces for motor-affected patients, gentle tempos for anxiety. Every decision is informed.
Step 04
Document & Return
Every performance is photographed, filmed, and logged. Audience reactions, coordinator feedback, what worked — all tracked. TAP builds recurring relationships, not one-off visits.
How It Started
One cold email.
One yes.
A monthly stage.
TAP started with a question that had no good answer: where do student musicians actually get to perform — not compete, not audition, just play for real people in a real place?

The first outreach went to ActivCare Senior Living in 2026. Two 9th graders asked if they could bring acoustic music to residents. The activities coordinator responded within days — not with a single slot, but with an invitation onto the monthly schedule. Orientation paperwork, health clearances — all handled by the founders themselves. That became the proof of concept. And the template for every venue that follows.
"What a giving heart you two have. Wonderful — I'd love to have you both on our monthly schedule."
— Eunice Wenger · ActivCare Senior Living · San Diego
